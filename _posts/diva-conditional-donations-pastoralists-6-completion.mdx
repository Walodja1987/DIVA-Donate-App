We are pleased to announce the successful completion of the **6th DIVA Donate campaign**, which ran from **October to December 2025** and introduced a **progressive, multi-checkpoint payout design** for anticipatory drought relief to pastoralist communities in **Laikipia North, Kenya**.

This campaign marked an important evolution for DIVA Donate: moving from a single end-of-season assessment to **nine NDVI observation windows**, enabling earlier and more frequent payouts when vegetation stress emerged during the rainy season.

In total, **four payouts were triggered**, demonstrating both the operational feasibility and the real-world relevance of progressive parametric cash transfers.

---

## ✨ Campaign overview

The objective of the 6th campaign remained unchanged:
to provide **timely, need-based cash support** to pastoralist households whose livelihoods depend on healthy pasture availability.

What changed fundamentally was **how and when payouts could occur**.

Instead of a single NDVI measurement at the end of the Oct–Dec rainy season, the campaign was structured around **nine ten-day checkpoints**, each linked to a separate payout pool and assessed against a fixed NDVI threshold of **0.55**.

If vegetation health dropped below this level at any checkpoint, the corresponding pool was released automatically.

---

## 🆕 Progressive payouts vs. the legacy single-window model

Under the **legacy single-window model**, the NDVI outcome observed during this season would have triggered a **full payout of the campaign amount**.

With the new **multi-window design**, payouts were more granular:

* **4 out of 9 checkpoints** fell below the threshold
* Each triggered window released **~4,074 RLUSD**
* Total payouts amounted to **4 × 4,074 = 16,296 RLUSD**

This difference is not a weakness of the new design—it is the intended outcome:

* The old model is **binary**: all or nothing, late in the season
* The new model is **responsive**: early support when stress appears, and restraint when conditions improve

This campaign thus provides a concrete, data-backed comparison between the two approaches.

---

## 📊 NDVI outcomes across the season

Out of the nine observation windows:

* **Windows 2, 3, 8 and 9** triggered payouts
* **Windows 1, 4, 5, 6 and 7** showed sufficient vegetation and did not trigger

Importantly, NDVI conditions fluctuated materially during the season—confirming that a single end-of-season snapshot can mask **early-season stress** that still has real consequences for herd recovery and resilience.

---

## ⚠️ Operational disruption: FEWS data interruption

During **Window 3**, outcome reporting was disrupted by the **US government shutdown**, which temporarily halted publication of NDVI data by **FEWS**, our primary data source.

Key implications:

* NDVI reporting for the 31 Oct snapshot was **delayed by 18 days**
* We explored alternative NDVI datasets, but:

  * Each dataset has its own calibration, resolution, and methodology
  * Values are **not directly comparable** without extensive normalization
* Ultimately, the value was reported once FEWS data became available again, using a **fallback reporting setup**

This incident highlights an important operational lesson:

> **Data availability risk exists even in satellite-based systems.**

This risk is **not specific to multi-window campaigns**—the same issue could have affected a single end-of-season snapshot. Going forward, a clearly defined **fallback data strategy** should be part of campaign design.

## 💸 Payout execution and beneficiary impact

### Summary of payouts

Across the four triggered windows:

* **16,296 RLUSD** were released
* Converted and disbursed as **KES 1,922,777.85**
* Reaching **506 unique pastoralists**
* **81% of recipients were women**

Each payout round was executed within **hours of outcome reporting**, demonstrating end-to-end automation from satellite data to mobile money (M-Pesa).

---

## 🐐 One large payout or several smaller ones?

The multi-window approach surfaced an important practical question:

### Would a single large payout be better for pastoralists?

**Observed positives of progressive payouts**

* Beneficiaries received money **quickly**, early in the season
* Immediate feedback reinforced trust: *“this thing works”*
* Reduced uncertainty compared to waiting months for a binary outcome

**Observed limitations**

* Individual payouts (~KES 1,000 per person) are often:

  * Too small to meaningfully finance **fodder purchases**
  * More likely to be used for **household consumption or transport**
* A larger lump sum would more likely be allocated toward **livestock-specific needs**

This suggests a potential trade-off:

> Progressive payouts improve **timing and trust**,
> while larger single payouts may better support **productive investments**.

Future campaign designs could explore **hybrid structures**, such as:

* Early micro-payouts + conditional end-of-season top-ups
* Accumulating triggered windows into a delayed bulk release

---

## 🏦 Liquidity management and operational feasibility

From an implementing partner perspective, the progressive model proved **manageable**:

* Individual payout rounds were relatively small
* Liquidity requirements did **not materially disrupt** Fortune Connect’s core operations
* Staggered payouts reduced peak liquidity pressure compared to a single large disbursement

This is an important validation point:
**progressive parametric aid is not only technically feasible, but operationally realistic** for local financial partners.

---

## 💰 Transaction costs and efficiency

Running four payout rounds instead of one naturally increased the number of transactions.

That said:

* **Total transaction costs** amounted to **~0.8%** of funds disbursed
* Costs included:

  * Blockchain transactions
  * FX conversion
  * Off-ramping
  * Mobile money fees

While slightly higher than a single payout, these costs remain **well within acceptable bounds**—especially considering the improved timing and resilience benefits.

(Quantifying the delta versus a hypothetical single-payout scenario is a next step.)

## 📚 Key learnings from Campaign 6

1. **Progressive payouts work operationally**
   End-to-end automation across multiple checkpoints is viable at low marginal cost.

2. **Timing matters as much as amount**
   Early-season stress is real and visible in NDVI—even if end-season conditions recover.

3. **Payout size influences usage**
   Smaller, frequent payouts support household liquidity; larger payouts better support livestock-specific interventions.

4. **Data dependency risk must be planned for**
   Even satellite-based systems require contingency planning.

5. **Liquidity impact on local partners is manageable**
   Progressive payouts do not inherently strain operational capacity.

## 🌖 Conclusion

The 6th DIVA Donate campaign represents a **milestone** for anticipatory action:

* It validates **progressive parametric aid** in a real-world setting
* It surfaces nuanced trade-offs between **timeliness, size, and impact**
* It demonstrates that blockchain-based infrastructure can support **complex payout logic** at low cost and high reliability

Most importantly, it reinforces the core mission of DIVA Donate:
to deliver **the right support, at the right time, based on objective data**.

We are deeply grateful to our partners and donors for enabling this experimentation—and to the pastoralist communities whose participation makes learning and improvement possible.

DIVA Donate is an innovative platform that leverages blockchain technology to enable parametric conditional donations for pre-disaster
