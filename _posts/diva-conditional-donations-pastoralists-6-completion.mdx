We are pleased to announce the successful completion of the **6th DIVA Donate campaign**, which ran from **October to December 2025** to provide anticipatory drought relief to pastoralist communities in **Laikipia North, Kenya**. This campaign was conducted in collaboration with **Ripple** and utilized their stablecoin, **RLUSD**, on Ethereum as the payout token to demonstrate its applicability in humanitarian aid. The partnership with Ripple is a testament to the value of our work and mission to build the most effective platform for disaster relief.

In this campaign, we **tested a multi-checkpoint design** as an alternative to the previous single end-of-season assessment. By utilizing **multiple NDVI checkpoints** throughout the rainy period, we explored the benefits of earlier and more frequent payouts to build resilience.

In total, **four payouts totaling $16,296 were triggered**, providing pastoralists financial support during times of low vegetation.

---

## ✨ Campaign overview

The objective of the 6th campaign remained unchanged: to provide **timely, need-based cash support** to pastoralist households whose livelihoods depend on healthy pasture availability.

Instead of a single NDVI measurement at the end of the Oct–Dec rainy season, the period was split into **nine checkpoints** spaced every 10 days, beginning on 10 October and ending on 31 December 2025. At each checkpoint, NDVI was assessed against a fixed threshold of **0.55** (refer to our [announcement article](https://www.divadonate.xyz/posts/diva-conditional-donations-pastoralists-6) for derivation details); if vegetation health dropped below this level, the corresponding pool was released automatically.


The total campaign funds of **\$36,673** were distributed evenly across the nine checkpoints, allocating **$4,074** to each.

## 🆕 Progressive payouts vs. single payout model

The last NDVI in the observation period was **0.49** on 31 December. In the **single payout model**, it would have triggered a payout of **\$36,673** (100%) at the end of the season (assuming a linear payout curve ranging from 0% payout at 0.55 NDVI to 100% at 0.50). This compares to **\$16,296** (c. 44%) in the **multi-window model**.

Although the total payout was lower this time in the multi-window model, the results could easily be reversed. In a late recovery scenario where vegetation starts poorly but recovers by the end of December the multi-window model would provide early-season support, whereas the single payout model would trigger no payout at all, ignoring the months of stress pastoralists endured.

Besides timing, another benefit of the progressive payouts is trust. By delivering smaller, more frequent payouts, beneficiaries see the model working rather than waiting months for a result.

## ⚠️ Operational disruption: FEWS data interruption

During **Window 3**, outcome reporting was disrupted by the **US government shutdown**, which temporarily halted publication of NDVI data by **FEWS**, our primary data source.

Key implications:

* NDVI reporting for the 31 Oct snapshot was **delayed by 18 days**
* We explored alternative NDVI datasets, but:

  * Each dataset has its own calibration, resolution, and methodology
  * Values are **not directly comparable** without extensive normalization
* Ultimately, the value was reported once FEWS data became available again, using a **fallback reporting setup**

This incident highlights an important operational lesson:

> **Data availability risk exists even in satellite-based systems.**

This risk is **not specific to multi-window campaigns**—the same issue could have affected a single end-of-season snapshot. Going forward, a clearly defined **fallback data strategy** should be part of campaign design.

## 💸 Payout execution and beneficiary impact

Below is a summary of the key campaign statistics:

* Amount disbursed: **\$16,296**
* Pastoralists reached: **506** ($32.20 per person)
* Female recipients: **81%**

The campaign demonstrated remarkable speed in execution. Excluding the period of data disruption during Window 3 (see [Key Learnings](#key-learnings) section for details), it took on average just **5–40 minutes** to report the NDVI outcome once the reporting window opened and another **1-5.5 hours** until funds were disbursed to pastoralists via M-Pesa. This represents a further improvement compared to the **14.5 hours** recorded during the campaign with Mercy Corps Ventures in Q4 2023. 

This efficiency gain was largely driven by the multi-window design, which allowed the team to perform and refine the disbursement process multiple times, leading to rapid operational improvements over the course of the season.

## 🐐 One large payout or several smaller ones?

The multi-window approach surfaced an important practical question:

### Would a single large payout be better for pastoralists?

**Observed positives of progressive payouts**

* Beneficiaries received money **quickly**, early in the season
* Immediate feedback reinforced trust: *“this thing works”*
* Reduced uncertainty compared to waiting months for a binary outcome

**Observed limitations**

* Individual payouts (~KES 1,000 per person) are often:

  * Too small to meaningfully finance **fodder purchases**
  * More likely to be used for **household consumption or transport**
* A larger lump sum would more likely be allocated toward **livestock-specific needs**

This suggests a potential trade-off:

> Progressive payouts improve **timing and trust**,
> while larger single payouts may better support **productive investments**.

Future campaign designs could explore **hybrid structures**, such as:

* Early micro-payouts + conditional end-of-season top-ups
* Accumulating triggered windows into a delayed bulk release

## 🏦 Liquidity management and operational feasibility

From an implementing partner perspective, the progressive model proved **manageable**:

* Individual payout rounds were relatively small
* Liquidity requirements did **not materially disrupt** Fortune Connect’s core operations
* Staggered payouts reduced peak liquidity pressure compared to a single large disbursement

This is an important validation point:
**progressive parametric aid is not only technically feasible, but operationally realistic** for local financial partners.

---

## 💰 Transaction costs and efficiency

Running four payout rounds instead of one naturally increased the number of transactions.

That said:

* **Total transaction costs** amounted to **~0.8%** of funds disbursed
* Costs included:

  * Blockchain transactions
  * FX conversion
  * Off-ramping
  * Mobile money fees

While slightly higher than a single payout, these costs remain **well within acceptable bounds**—especially considering the improved timing and resilience benefits.

## 📚 Key learnings from Campaign 6

1. **Progressive payouts work operationally**
   End-to-end automation across multiple checkpoints is viable at low marginal cost.

2. **Timing matters as much as amount**
   Early-season stress is real and visible in NDVI—even if end-season conditions recover.

3. **Payout size influences usage**
   Smaller, frequent payouts support household liquidity; larger payouts better support livestock-specific interventions.

4. **Data dependency and comparability risks**
   The campaign highlighted a heavy reliance on centralized data providers (like FEWS). NDVI data aggregation is highly complex and not easily replicable; different sources (e.g., EU Copernicus) use varying logic and resolution, making datasets difficult to compare or use as immediate fallbacks without extensive normalization.

5. **Liquidity impact on local partners is manageable**
   Progressive payouts do not inherently strain operational capacity and can even reduce peak liquidity pressure.
